— Public Forum —

Overview

Public Forum debate is a type of current events debate which is a widespread form of high school debate in the U.S and it was designed to be accessible to the average citizen. Individuals give short (4 minute) speeches that are interspersed with 3 minute "Crossfire" sections, questions and answers between opposed debaters. The winner is determined by a judge who also serves as a referee (timing sections, penalizing incivility, etc). The debate centers around advocating or rejecting a position, "resolve", or "resolution", which is usually a proposal of a potential solution to a current events issue. Public Forum tournaments typically have 4-7 preliminary rounds of which the best teams break to elimination rounds to compete for Bids to the Tournament of Champions.

Format

(3 mins Prep Time) *Aff and Neg are interchangable

  • Aff Case (1A) - 4 mins
  • Neg Case (1N) - 4 mins
  • Cross Fire (1A x 1N) - 3 mins
  • Aff Rebuttal (2A) - 4 mins
  • Neg Rebuttal (2N) - 4 mins
  • Cross Fire (2A x 2N) - 3 mins
  • Aff Summary (1A) - 3 mins
  • Neg Summary (1N) - 3 mins
  • Grand Cross Fire (everyone) - 3 mins
  • Aff Final Focus (2A) - 2 mins
  • Neg Final Focus (2N) - 2 mins

Schedule

Unlike the other formats South competes in, PF is not limited to any one tournament, but rather it is the format we compete in for most of our tournaments. South competes in many elite national circuit tournaments from the Princeton Classic to the Upenn Invitational, but typically we have 3 seasons of competition: fall, winter, and spring. The fall season takes place from November to December and includes the following tournaments: Princeton, Ridge, Yale, Capitol, etc. The winter season takes place from late January to the end of February and includes the following tournaments: Upenn, Pennsbury, Harvard, Lakeland, etc. The spring season is typically only open to seasoned debaters as it only includes selective championship tournaments including Districts, States, and the Tournament of Champions.
