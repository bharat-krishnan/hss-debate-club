CVC

— Colonial Valley Conference —

Overview

CVC debate is a policy debate competition where a team either presents a plan supporting a topic, called the Resolved, or rebuts the opposing team's plan. The Affirmative side is the team that creates a plan that in some way must fulfill the Resolved. They explain their plan, and argue why their plan is effective and relevant. The Negative side listens to the Affirmative’s plan, and has to rebut it by finding the holes in the plan, and discussing the ineffectiveness of it.

Format

(3 mins of Prep Time)

  • 1st Aff Constructive (1AC) - 8 mins
  • 2nd Neg Cross Fire (2NX) - 3 mins
  • 1st Neg Constructive (1NC) - 8 mins
  • 1st Aff Cross Fire (1AX) - 3 mins
  • 2nd Aff Constructive (2AC) - 8 mins
  • 1st Neg Cross Fire (1NX) - 3 mins
  • 2nd Neg Constructive (2NC) - 8 mins
  • 2nd Aff Cross Fire (2AX) - 3 mins
  • 2nd Neg Constructive (2NC) - 8 mins
  • 2nd Aff Cross Fire (2AX) - 3 mins
  • Neg Rebuttal (1N or 2N) - 4 mins
  • Aff Rebuttal (1A or 2A) - 3 mins

Schedule

CVC is the first format that members are exposed to and it always takes place through the fall every year. The tournament spans 8 or so weeks, and on every Thursday, South travels to a competing school to compete. The tournament has 2 divisions Varsity and JV, and while varsity is incredibly selective, the JV competitors change every week allowing many members to compete over the course of the tournament.
