PMC

— Princeton Moot Court —

Overview

Moot court is a simulation of an appeals court or Supreme Court hearing. Teams of two “attorneys” prepare and present arguments on a legal case (like the constitutionality of health care) to a judge or judge panel. You’ll learn real case law, legal precedent, and how to argue like a Constitutional lawyer.

Format

(Not Accounting for Questions)

  • Petitioner Case - 12-14 mins
  • Respondent Case + Rebuttal - 20 mins
  • Petitioner Rebuttal - 6-8 mins

Schedule

PMC takes place in the Spring of every year, most commonly in April. The tournament itself has 4 preliminary rounds over 2 days, and only the top 16 teams break into elimination rounds.
